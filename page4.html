Mobirise is an offline app for Window and Mac to easily create small/medium websites, landing pages, online resumes and portfolios, promo sites for apps, events, services and products.

It was almost inevitable, from the moment I enrolled in General Assembly's amazingly intense 3 month Data Science immersive, that my capstone project would, in one way or another, be related to health and wellbeing. Mental health is a bit of an obsession of mine so while my course may now be complete, it is highly likely I will return to this project from time to time as the whirlwind of information from the course takes root in my brain and as I continue to build on what I have learnt so far. Reader beware, you may well get tired of hearing about this project ;)

When I found OSMI's datasets I was jubilant. Publicly available data bridging two of my key interests! How quickly I had forgotten what an alumni had told us early on in the course - "The data is always dirty" and was it ever! We were also told that 80% of data science is cleaning and EDA (exploratory data analysis) but this was a very conservative figure in my experience. Ultimately I had to find a point where the data was 'clean enough', I'm still itching to go back and clean it some more.

Of course, what you really want to know is what insights I found. This graph shows reported impact on productivity. Blue is the responses indicating mental health issues never interfered with their work and oranges is all responses indicating any impact whether frequent or infrequent.
